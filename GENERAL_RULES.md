## Purpose
Casino Mayor is a heritage venue concept in Candelario where culture, entertainment, hospitality, and social nightlife are intended to come together under one historic roof.

## Program Scope
- Theatre & Dance: live performances, dance courses led by renowned artists, and social dance programming.
- Mountain & Outdoor: hiking-focused stays, bouldering-related experiences, and outdoor-led programming connected to the Sierra setting.
- Social Nightlife: disco restaurant format, tapas bar, and pool billiard.
- Casino: a dedicated gaming floor with its own cocktail bar, on the first floor.
- Hospitality: two apartments available for short and medium stays.
- Restaurant: full dining experience, with wine drawn directly from the venue's own bodega.
- Cinema: film programming as part of the cultural offering.
- Latin Events: Salsa, Bachata, and Kizomba evenings and courses.

## Building Layout
- Lower level: the Theatre — the main dance and performance stage, with cinema wall and clear sightlines; plus a bodega on the same level.
- Street level: the Bar — a smaller room suitable for private workshops, small-group dance classes, and tapas billiard evenings.
- First floor: the Casino — the name is kept for identity purposes; the actual use is TBD (most likely a second dance floor or a bar format). Has its own cocktail bar area.
- Second floor: two apartments — Apartment GardenView and Apartment StreetView.
- Upper level: the Atico.

### Multi-Room Dance Configuration
On dance evenings, all three rooms can run in parallel: the Theatre holds the main class, the first-floor room (Casino in name) accommodates a second level group, and the Bar hosts small private workshops. Up to 3 classes per evening per room.

> **Note:** The Casino room will likely not operate as a traditional casino. The name is part of the building's identity. Its use — dance floor or bar — is still to be decided.

## Yearly Calendar

Candelario has four anchor celebrations each year. Casino Mayor plans its major seasonal programming around these dates.

### 2 February — La Candelaria
Marks the end of the chacinera (sausage-making) season. The village celebrates with a probadura de chichas — a communal tasting of freshly made charcuterie — followed by a procession. Casino Mayor marks this with a **Bodega & Charcuterie Evening**: local embutido paired with wine from the bodega, served in the Bar. The Theatre may host a folk music set.

### 1 May — Cristo del Refugio
Community procession from the Ermita del Humilladero to the parish church, followed by a novena. Casino Mayor uses this as the seasonal reopening moment: a **Heritage Spring Evening** on the Theatre stage, with acoustic programming and a curated bodega menu.

### 26 July — Santa Ana (Fiestas Mayores)
The patron saint festivities: four days of music, culture, and celebration. The largest visitor influx of the year. Casino Mayor runs its peak programme across all rooms: headline concerts on the Theatre stage, all three dance rooms in full operation, Casino open late, and a **Gala Stage Dinner** on the evening of 26 July. Reservations essential.

### Second Sunday of August — La Boda Típica
A theatrical recreation of a 19th-century Candelario wedding, declared a Festival of Regional Tourist Interest. The village fills with visitors in traditional dress. Casino Mayor hosts a complementary **Heritage Gala Evening**: live music, a traditional dance exhibition on stage, and a themed bodega menu — a cultural counterpart to the festivity in the streets.

### Semana Santa (March/April — dates vary)
Holy Week brings cultural visitors to Candelario. Casino Mayor programmes cinema screenings, acoustic sets, and a curated wine tasting across the Easter break. Lighter format, earlier opening hours.
